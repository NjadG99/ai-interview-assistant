üìå Interview Questions + Answers
    
1. Q: How do you configure a self-hosted agent pool?
   A: Configuring a self-hosted agent pool involves setting up agents on your own infrastructure for CI/CD tasks.

2. Q: How do you write a CI/CD YAML pipeline?
   A: A CI/CD YAML pipeline automates the software development lifecycle, enabling continuous integration and delivery.

3. Q: What is a service in Kubernetes?
   A: Kubernetes services enable communication between pods and external resources, managing access and load balancing.

4. Q: Write a Dockerfile to create a Docker image?
   A: A Dockerfile is a text document that contains all the commands a user could call on the command line to assemble an image.

5. Q: How do you check logs for pods and containers in Kubernetes?
   A: To check logs for pods and containers in Kubernetes, you can use the kubectl command-line tool.

6. Q: Explain Monitoring in DevOps.
   A: Monitoring in DevOps is the process of observing and measuring the performance of the system to ensure its availability and reliability.

7. Q: What DevOps tools have you worked on?
   A: I have worked with various DevOps tools including Jenkins, Git, Docker, Kubernetes, Ansible, and AWS.

8. Q: What do you understand about nested classes?
   A: Object-oriented programming languages allow defining a class inside another class. The enclosed class is the nested class. It has the same access rights as other class members serving as another member of the outer class.
 
9. Q: What is cloud computing? How is it useful today?
   A: Cloud computing is the technique of storing, processing, and managing data in a shared network of remote servers over the internet. The data is distributed and hence more secure. Cloud computing offers flexible resources, innovation, and economies of scale.

10.Q: What is Big Data?
   A: Big data refers to large data sets too complex for traditional data-processing application software.

üí° Tips
- Be prepared to discuss in detail any projects, skills, or certifications listed. For DevOps roles, this often means walking through your experience with CI/CD pipelines, automation, or specific tools.
- Gain hands-on experience with tools like Terraform for automating infrastructure setup and management.
- Deeply understand at least one major cloud platform, such as AWS, and its relevant services for scalability and resource flexibility. 
- Master Docker for containerization and Kubernetes for orchestrating containerized applications, including concepts like StatefulSets versus Deployments and managing the kublet.
- Become adept in at least one programming language and master scripting for automation.
- Understand how to operate and manage operating systems, including working with the terminal.
- Develop excellent communication skills to bridge the gap between development and operations teams and collaborate effectively with architects and developers.
- Cultivate strong analytical and troubleshooting skills to quickly diagnose and resolve issues, ensuring minimal downtime.
- Be ready to discuss real-time scenarios and how you'd apply DevOps principles and tools to solve them.
