üí° Tips
- Be proficient in Kotlin and/or Java, as these are the standard languages for Android development. 
- Understand and be prepared to explain common architectures like MVVM (Model-View-ViewModel) and Clean Architecture, which promote code organization and maintainability. 
- ConstraintLayout: For building flexible and efficient user interfaces.
- Be aware of best practices like the Single Responsibility Principle (SRP).
- Keep your resume concise and to the point, as recruiters spend little time on it. 
- Be ready to discuss the projects listed on your resume, explaining the technology choices, architecture used, and your contributions.
- Stay updated on current trends in technology, such as AI and machine learning, and be prepared to discuss them. 
- Project confidence and a positive attitude during the interview. 
- Develop strong communication skills to interact effectively with team members and stakeholders. 
- Be prepared to demonstrate your problem-solving skills during technical interviews.

- Blocking the main thread: Performing intensive operations like network requests, database queries, or heavy bitmap processing on the main (UI) thread causes "Application Not Responding" (ANR) errors and a poor user experience.
- Neglecting the Android lifecycle: Failing to manage the lifecycle of Activities and Fragments correctly can cause memory leaks and crashes, especially during configuration changes like screen rotations.
